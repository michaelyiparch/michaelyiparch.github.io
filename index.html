About

"Architecture must breathe — especially in the densest city on Earth."

I'm Michael Yip (Yip Hoi Kwong), a Year 3 architecture student at Chu Hai College. My work explores high-density urban housing through computational design, modular construction, and environmental simulation. I use Revit, Rhino, Grasshopper, and Adobe Suite to produce work that is technically grounded and spatially ambitious.

Year 3 Studio · 2026

The Aero-Matrix

A high-rise that breathes. Modular construction reimagined as a porous infrastructure — restoring wind, light, and community to the densest urban fabric in the world.

Location
San Sau Street, Tuen Mun
Program
Modular High-Rise Housing
Height
57m · 15 Floors
Construction
MiC · Modular Integrated
Status
Academic Studio Project
The Private Unit — compact, efficient, flooded with natural light and fresh air through the full-height window
Left: The Communal Heart — a shared kitchen and living space where residents gather.  ·  Right: The Lounge — an elevated social pocket with views across Tuen Mun.

Across four interconnected spaces — the Modular Void, the Private Unit, the Communal Heart, and the Lounge — the Aero-Matrix demonstrates that high-density living can be generous, light-filled, and breathable. Each single-height space is calibrated for cross-ventilation, daylight, and a sense of home.

Typical Floor Plan
Mirrored MiC modules arranged around a central core — hollow slots create continuous air channels through the entire building
