About

Michael Yip is an architecture student at Chu Hai College of Higher Education in Hong Kong, working at the intersection of computational design, modular construction, and environmental performance.

His practice investigates how architecture can reconcile the extreme density of the contemporary city with spaces of generosity, light, and breath. Proficient in Revit, Rhino, Grasshopper, and the Adobe Suite, with developing expertise in CFD simulation and parametric workflows — he approaches each project as both a technical challenge and a spatial proposition, grounded in the specific urban conditions of Hong Kong.

The Aero-Matrix

Hong Kong's "Wall Effect" blocks wind and traps heat. The Aero-Matrix responds with a breathing high-rise — strategic voids become wind portals, restoring natural ventilation to the city.

Location
San Sau Street, Tuen Mun
Program
Modular High-Rise Housing
Height
57m · 15 Floors
Construction
MiC · Modular Integrated
Status
Academic Studio Project
The Hollow Module — voids in the grid become breathing pockets for wind and social life.
The Private Unit — compact MiC living with a large window opening to the balcony for natural light and breeze.
The Communal Heart — shared kitchen and living, open to the hollow modules for constant fresh air.
The Lounge — social space with passive cooling and unobstructed city views.
Typical Floor Plan
Typical floor — mirrored MiC modules minimize production types, maximize cross-ventilation.

Through modular innovation, we build faster and more efficiently — creating a breathing architecture that benefits both the occupants and the city.
